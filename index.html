Japanese

Tonkotsu Ramen

A soul-warming bowl of rich, creamy pork-bone broth simmered for 12 hours, topped with chashu pork belly, a soft-boiled marinated egg, bamboo shoots, nori and spring onions — Japan's ultimate comfort food.

🕒 3 hrs 👤 2 servings 📊 Hard ⭐ 4.9 / 5

Key Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork trotters & neck bones
  • 2 packs fresh ramen noodles
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs, marinated
  • Chashu pork belly, sliced
  • Bamboo shoots (menma)
  • Nori sheets & spring onions
  • Sesame oil, miso, soy sauce

Quick Steps

  1. Blanch bones, rinse, then simmer 10–12 hrs for broth
  2. Season broth with miso and soy
  3. Prepare chashu by rolling & braising pork belly
  4. Marinate soft-boiled eggs in soy mixture overnight
  5. Cook noodles per package, drain
  6. Assemble bowl and serve piping hot
